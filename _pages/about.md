I am a Ph.D. Candidate (expected graduation: September 2026) in the joint program between **City University of Hong Kong** (Department of Information Systems) and **Xi'an Jiaotong University** (School of Management).

My research investigates how shared mobility platforms reshape urban ecosystems through **cross-market spillover effects**. Using quasi-experimental methods (DiD, IV, RDD) and large-scale urban data, I study how the entry of sharing-economy platforms generates unintended consequences for incumbent markets, consumer welfare, and public safety. My work spans **empirical operations management**, **information systems**, and **urban computing**, with a focus on bike-sharing, home-sharing, taxi markets, restaurants, urban crime, and traffic safety.

**Research Interests:** Sharing Economy, Empirical Operations Management, Urban Computing, Platform Economics
